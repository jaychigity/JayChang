# AdvisorJay.com — Content Strategy
# Last updated: April 2026

## Purpose
This file documents the content strategy for advisorjay.com — what to publish,
in what order, and why. It works alongside PUBLISHED-CONTENT.md (which tracks

## The Strategy in One Sentence

Every piece of content exists to answer a specific question a potential client
is already asking — and to position Jay as the person they should talk to next.

## Target Audiences (in priority order)

### Tier 1 — Primary (build content for these first)
1. **Families, retirees, and widows** — Life transition moments, estate questions,
   retirement income planning. Often $1M-5M in assets.
2. **Telecom & utility employees (blue collar)** — AT&T, PG&E, SRP. Ages 55+,
   $1M-3M, approaching retirement. Pension decisions, 401(k) optimization.
3. **Nonprofit & institutional** — Board members, executives, endowment managers.
   403(b)/457(b) questions, fiduciary duties.

### Tier 2 — Secondary (build after Tier 1 foundation is solid)
4. **Aerospace & defense employees** — Raytheon, Honeywell, Boeing. RSU/equity,
   early retirement packages, security clearance considerations.
5. **Physicians & healthcare executives** — High income, complex tax situations,
   deferred compensation, practice transitions.
6. **Tech & semiconductor professionals** — RSU concentration, IPO planning,
   stock option timing.

### Tier 3 — Expand over time
7. **Fortune 500 & corporate employees** — General corporate benefits optimization
8. **High net worth individuals** — $5M+, estate planning, alternative investments
9. **Business owners & entrepreneurs** — Exit planning, succession, key person insurance

## Content Types

### 1. Tool & Calculator Pages (`/tools/[slug]`)
- Lead magnets — free, instant value, no sign-up required
- Drive organic traffic through specific long-tail keywords
- Each tool links to related service page and articles
- See TOOLS-CONVENTION.md for build specs

### 2. Company-Specific Guides (`/guides/[company]-employee-benefits`)
- Highest priority content after tools
- Target employees searching for benefits help at their specific company
- Include: benefits overview, pension/401k details, common mistakes, Jay's expertise
- Link to relevant calculators and service pages
- Build for AT&T and PG&E first, then expand

### 3. Learn Articles (`/learn/[topic-slug]`)
- Educational content that answers specific questions
- Organized into clusters: 401(k) & Retirement, RSU & Equity, Tax Planning,
  Life Transitions, Honest Guides
- Each article targets one primary keyword
- 800-1500 words, scannable with clear H2/H3 structure
- Every article ends with a warm CTA to talk to Jay

### 4. Sector Pages (`/[industry]-wealth-management`)
- Landing pages for each client type Jay serves
- SEO-optimized for "[industry] wealth management" searches
- Include: who this is for, common challenges, how Jay helps, relevant tools, CTA
- Already built for 6 sectors; 3 more planned

### 5. Glossary (`/glossary`)
- One page with anchor-linked definitions
- Targets "what is [term]" searches
- Each definition is 2-4 sentences, plain language, links to relevant article
- Start with 10-15 core terms, expand over time

### 6. Location Pages (`/[city]`)
- Target "[city] financial advisor" searches
- Already built for Scottsdale, Las Vegas, California, Areas We Serve

## Content Rules

### Voice
Follow VOICE-CONVENTION.md for all copy. Quick reference:
- "I" for Jay's personal relationship with clients
- "We" for Farther's platform and team resources
- Warm, direct, no corporate filler

### No gated content
All articles, guides, and tools are free and ungated.
Strategy: radical transparency builds more trust and organic leads than email gates.

### No company logos
Do not display employer logos (AT&T, PG&E, etc.) on any page.
Reason: SEC/FINRA compliance concerns and broader audience appeal.
Use text-based sector framing instead.

### Evergreen first
Write content that stays relevant for 12+ months. Date-specific content
(e.g., "2026 limits") gets updated annually — see TOOLS-CONVENTION.md for
the annual update checklist.

### Inclusive language when naming employers
On **service pages, location pages, and sector pages** — any page that describes who
Jay serves broadly — always use "professionals" when pairing a company name with a
role description. Never use narrow job titles like "engineers", "physicians", "executives",
or "doctors" next to a specific employer name on these pages. This ensures we don't
exclude anyone at that organization.

- ✅ "TSMC professionals" / "Banner Health professionals" / "Honeywell professionals"
- ❌ "TSMC engineers" / "Banner Health physicians" / "Honeywell executives"

**Exception — Insight articles and guides**: Articles that target a specific audience
with specific content (e.g., "TSMC RSU Tax Planning for Arizona Engineers" or
"Banner Health Physician Tax Strategies") may use role-specific titles because the
content genuinely applies to that role. The inclusive language rule is about how we
describe Jay's *audience* on pages that position his services broadly.

**Exception — Service descriptions**: Describing a type of service is fine even on
broad pages (e.g., "equity compensation planning", "physician tax strategies").
The rule applies to how we refer to *people*, not *services*.

### Compliance
- Every calculator includes a disclaimer (see TOOLS-CONVENTION.md)
- No performance promises or guarantees
- No specific investment recommendations in public content
- Always include "past performance does not guarantee future results" where relevant
- Jay's title: "VP, Wealth Advisor at Farther Finance, Inc."
